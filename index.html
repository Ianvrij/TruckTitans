Legislation and Compliance

The European Union has established stringent CO2 emission reduction targets for new commercial vehicles under Regulation (EU) 2019/1242:

  • 15% reduction in CO2 emissions by 2025 compared to 2019 levels.
  • 30% reduction in CO2 emissions by 2030 compared to 2019 levels.

Non-compliance can result in significant penalties. For more information, visit the European Union Law website.

Many European cities have implemented LEZs and ZEZs to reduce pollution. Non-compliant vehicles may face fines or access restrictions. Learn more at Urban Access Regulations Europe.

Various subsidies and tax incentives are available to support fleet electrification. For example, the Dutch SEBA offers up to €5,000 per electric vehicle. Visit the RVO Subsidy Portal for details.

Countries have set ambitious electrification targets to meet the European Green Deal's objectives. Stay informed to ensure compliance and benefit from incentives. More info at the European Commission website.

We offer tailored compliance reports to guide your fleet's electrification process, ensuring you meet all regulatory requirements while optimizing financial benefits. Contact us for a consultation.

Fuel Options

Electric Vehicles

Electric vehicles are at the core of fleet electrification, offering zero emissions and lower operating costs.

Hydrotreated Vegetable Oil (HVO)

HVO is a renewable diesel alternative that reduces emissions and can be used in existing diesel engines.

Diesel

Traditional diesel is becoming less favorable due to strict emission regulations and higher environmental impact.

Other Alternatives

Explore emerging technologies like hydrogen fuel cells and biofuels for sustainable fleet operations.
